# Alfred Scholar UX Principles

Bhushan's product design principles for Alfred Scholar. Apply them to every feature, screen, copy, and review touching the product.

1. Every AI answer must be traceable to evidence. No AI output without visible, verifiable provenance (citations, source links, honest "couldn't verify" states).
2. Reduce cognitive load by suggesting the next action. Screens, empty states, and completions should surface one clear next step.
3. Keep users inside a single research workspace. Avoid flows that push users out to other tools or tabs; integrate instead.
4. Make organization automatic whenever possible. Prefer auto-filing, auto-tagging, and sensible defaults over manual housekeeping.
5. Show progress and coverage, not just chat. Long-running or multi-source work (syncs, Telescope, analyses) must show what is done, what is pending, and what was covered or skipped.
6. Design for long sessions of focused work. Calm motion (fast, subtle, reduced-motion friendly), no unsolicited interruptions during reading or writing, comfortable contrast for hours of screen time.
7. Prefer clean typography and whitespace over visual effects. Restraint is the trust signal for this audience; no hype-coded decoration.
8. Success states celebrate subtly. Acknowledge completions with quiet, tasteful confirmation, not confetti or loud fanfare that breaks focus.
9. Errors guide rather than punish. Error states explain what happened, why, and the next step to recover; never blame the user or dead-end them.
